About Me

I'm currently studying computer science and math at Valparaiso University, where I'm learning not just how to program, but also how to manage and plan software projects with a team or by myself. My experience with Java and native apps, Python and data science, and web programming combine to form an adaptable skillset. A strong drive to identify problems and solve them with programming leads me to always seek out new skills to learn and challenges to tackle.

This determination to put my coding knowledge to work helped me succeed this past summer at two different internships. After picking up PHP and combining it with my HTML and CSS skills, I helped Journey Websites redesign a large client's WordPress site, which was previously laggy and bloated with tons of redundant plugins. Meanwhile, I was able to put my knowledge of chess to work at Y Stem and Chess, where I integrated the Stockfish engine into the Angular build so that students can practice without an opponent.

My love of learning and tackling challenging problems extends beyond my work and studies. In my free time, I love to play chess on lichess.org and to read about all sorts of topics, from computing to economics, in addition to occasional novels. I'm currently looking for internship or cooperative education opportunities. Make sure to send me a message if you'd like to connect!
